Sample Itinerary

July 16 – Arrival

Check into a charming Victorian inn. Stroll through downtown and enjoy a welcome dinner at a vegan-friendly café.

July 17 – Historic Tour

Visit the Eureka Springs Historical Museum and take a walking tour of local architecture. End the day with a ghost tour!

July 18 – Art Day

Explore art galleries and studios. Attend a local art class or workshop. Catch a live performance in the evening.

July 19 – Nature & Gem Mining

Go hiking on nearby trails and picnic by the springs. In the afternoon, visit a local gem mine like Blue Ribbon Gem Mining to discover quartz, amethyst, and other treasures.

Romantic Things to Do

  • Horse-drawn carriage rides through the historic district
  • Watch the sunset from the Crescent Hotel gardens
  • Picnic at Lake Leatherwood under shaded trees
  • Couples massage at a local spa
  • Write a love note and tuck it into a hidden spot along a nature trail

Events in Late July

During your stay, check out local events that celebrate summer and creativity:

  • Eureka Springs Farmers Market (Thursdays) – Fresh produce and handmade goods
  • Drumming in the Park (Weekends) – Join or watch local drummers in Basin Spring Park
  • Evening Concert Series – Outdoor music events downtown

Charming Places to Stay

  • 1886 Crescent Hotel & Spa – Historic and rumored to be haunted, with sunset views and spa services
  • Treehouse Cottages – Cozy, secluded cottages surrounded by nature
  • The Grand Treehouse Resort – Unique architecture and luxury touches for couples
  • Airbnb Options – Look for romantic lofts, cabins, or cottages near the springs

Hidden Gems & Local Tips

  • Visit Books on the White River, a quaint bookstore tucked into a hillside
  • Find the secret garden path near Spring Street
  • Grab a bench and people-watch at Basin Spring Park
  • Ask locals about the “healing springs” – some are hidden just off the main trails
  • Explore antique stores early in the morning for the best finds

Charm & Quirky Characters

Don’t miss the Working Bunnies—rescued rabbits like Mochi who “help” at shops like East by West and Caroline’s Collectables. Since 2000, they’ve been handing out change, receipts, pens, and bags to visitors—earning hearts worldwide!

Visitors say:
“They hand you your change/card back and your receipt… They’re the cutest!”

Gem & Mineral Adventures

Just a short drive from Eureka Springs, you can enjoy hands-on gem and mineral mining experiences at places like Blue Ribbon Gem Mining and Quigley's Castle. Discover quartz crystals, amethyst, and other local treasures — a sparkling activity for all ages!
